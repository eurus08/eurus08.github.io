About me

I am a master's graduate student in the Scientific Computing and Industrial Modelling (SCIM) programme at the National Institute for Mathematical Sciences (NIMS), Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST). My research interests span scientific computing, high-performance computing, and numerical analysis, with additional expertise in machine learning, optimisation, and graph theory. I have worked on a variety of research projects, including:

  • Graph theory and optimisation for evacuation planning: developing dynamic evacuation strategies for building complexes using graph-based models.
  • Modified primal simplex method for task allocation: creating an optimised framework for distributing tasks among employees efficiently.
  • Research assistantship at NIMS: supporting ongoing projects in computational mathematics and applied modelling.


I am passionate about applying advanced mathematical and computational techniques to solve real-world problems, bridging the gap between theory and practice. My long-term goal is to contribute to cutting-edge research in computational mathematics while also developing scalable solutions for industry.


Beyond academia,

I work part-time at Adepts Limited, Ghana, a software development company, as a database developer. In this role, I design, build, and maintain databases to support enterprise-level applications. I have strong proficiency in a wide range of programming and scripting languages, database management systems, and modern computational tools.
